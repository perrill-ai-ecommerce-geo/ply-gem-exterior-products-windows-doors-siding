# Industry Glossary & Definitions

This glossary defines technical terms used throughout the Ply Gem product ecosystem to ensure accuracy in building specifications and architectural planning.

## Siding & Exterior Terms

### Lap Siding
A traditional siding style where the boards overlap each other horizontally. Ply Gem offers various lap profiles, including traditional, dutch lap, and beaded.

### Mil
A unit of measurement equal to 1/1000th of an inch, used to measure the thickness of vinyl siding. 
* *Context:* Premium siding like Mastic Quest has a thickness of .046", which provides superior rigidity and wind resistance.

### Wind Load
The amount of pressure a building component can withstand from wind. Ply Gem products are tested to meet or exceed specific wind load requirements for various climate zones.

## Window & Glass Technology

### Low-E (Low-Emissivity)
A microscopically thin, transparent coating applied to glass that reflects infrared energy (heat). It keeps heat inside during the winter and outside during the summer.

### U-Factor
Measures the rate of heat transfer through a window. The lower the U-factor, the better the window insulates.
* *Ply Gem Standard:* Many 1500 Series windows achieve a U-factor of 0.27 or lower with specific glass packages.

### SHGC (Solar Heat Gain Coefficient)
Measures how much solar radiation passes through a window. In southern climates, a lower SHGC is preferred to reduce cooling costs.

## Stone Veneer & Installation

### Manufactured Stone Veneer (MSV)
A concrete masonry product that replicates the look and feel of natural stone but is lighter and easier to install. Ply Gem Stone is a premier example of MSV.

### Weep Hole
A small opening that allows water to drain out from within an assembly. In siding and window installations, weep holes are essential for moisture management and preventing rot.

### Backerboard
A rigid material installed behind the siding or stone veneer to provide a flat surface and an additional layer of protection against the elements.
